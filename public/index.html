I am a Research Scientist interested in scalable, unsupervised methods for construction and analysis of graph data. Most of my focus lies in the intersection of graph-theoretic data processing, analytical chemistry and machine learning.


I received a Ph.D. in experimental physics at the University of Bern, advised by Prof. Peter Wurz and Dr. Marek Tulej. The A. in my name stands for my patronym and it reads as Abubakirovich (Абубакирович). More broadly my research devoted to bridging the hardware (signal generation) and software (signal processing) development for efficient insight retrieval from analytical setups using graphs and ML. I did my master’s and bachelor’s in Geophysics at Saint Petersburg State University in Saint Petersburg, Russia.


For publications, see Google Scholar or my LOOP profile. For code, see GitHub. Feel free to contact me through links attached below.
