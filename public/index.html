I am a Research Scientist interested in scalable, unsupervised methods for construction and analysis of graph data. Most of my focus lies in the intersection of analytical chemistry and graph-theoretic data processing. Currently, dabbling in the world of entrepreneurship


I received a Ph.D. in experimental physics at the University of Bern, advised by Prof. Peter Wurz and Dr. Marek Tulej. The A. in my name stands for my patronym and it reads as Abubakirovich (Абубакирович). Considerable part of my research is about creating graph-structured objects from chemical/sensor data and efficient mining of the structural organization of such objects. More broadly my research devoted to bridging the hardware (signal generation) and software (signal processing) development for fast and efficient insight retrieval from analytical setups. I did my master’s and bachelor’s in Geophysics at Saint Petersburg State University in Saint Petersburg, Russia.


For publications, see Google Scholar or my LOOP profile. For code, see GitHub. Feel free to contact me through links attached below.
